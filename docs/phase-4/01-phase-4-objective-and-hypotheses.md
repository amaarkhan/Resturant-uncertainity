# Phase 4 Objective and Hypotheses

## Objective
Evaluate whether the Phase 3 MVP shows meaningful signs of Product-Market Fit (PMF) using measured user behavior, business impact, and willingness-to-pay signals.

## Core Question
Does the MVP create enough repeatable value for small restaurant owners to adopt, trust, and potentially pay for it?

## Hypotheses
1. Behavior hypothesis
- Owners will use the recommendation flow consistently (habit signal).

2. Value hypothesis
- Recommendations reduce waste and stockout rates versus baseline.

3. Trust hypothesis
- Users maintain confidence in recommendations over multiple weeks.

4. Commercial hypothesis
- A meaningful subset of pilot users shows willingness to pay.

## Scope of Phase 4
Included:
- Metric tracking and PMF decision gate.
- Pilot experiment and cohort analysis.
- Early go-to-market signal tests.

Not included:
- Large-scale geographic expansion.
- Enterprise feature set.
- Full pricing optimization at scale.

## Exit Criteria
- PMF decision made using predefined thresholds.
- Next action selected: scale, iterate, or pivot.
